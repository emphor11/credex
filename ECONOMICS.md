# Economics

Draft status: started on Day 1. Numbers below are placeholders for a spreadsheet-style model that will be refined after interviews and Credex assumptions.

Assumption: a converted Credex lead buys discounted AI credits with enough margin to make high-savings accounts valuable. If a startup spends $3,000/month on AI infrastructure and Credex can help shift even $1,500/month of that through discounted credits, a 10-20% gross margin would imply $150-$300/month in gross profit, or $1,800-$3,600/year per converted customer.

For the tool to work, the funnel has to be efficient: audit completed to email capture, email capture to consultation booked, consultation booked to credit purchase. A rough Day 1 target model is 100 audits, 35 captured reports, 7 consultations, and 1-2 purchases. If acquisition is founder-led and unpaid, CAC is mostly time cost. Paid CAC is intentionally out of scope for the first month.

The $1M ARR path likely requires the tool to identify larger accounts: about 280 customers worth $300/month in gross profit, or fewer customers if larger AI credit purchases are common. The key unknown is not traffic; it is the rate at which completed audits reveal credible savings above $500/month.
