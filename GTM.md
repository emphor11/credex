# GTM

Draft status: started on Day 1. This will be completed after user interviews.

The initial target user is a technical founder or engineering manager at a seed to Series A startup with 5-50 employees, at least 3 engineers, and visible AI tool usage across coding, writing, research, or customer support. They are likely paying for several overlapping tools because individual team members expensed them before anyone centralized the spend.

Before wanting this tool, they might be reading a monthly card statement, a Ramp/Brex software spend report, a Slack thread about AI budgets, or a post about Cursor/Claude/OpenAI pricing changes. The wedge is not "save money" in the abstract; it is "am I quietly paying retail for duplicate AI capability?"

Likely channels to test: founder-led posts on X, Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, r/startups, AI Engineer communities, local startup WhatsApp groups, university founder groups, and direct replies to pricing-change discussions. The first version should be shared as a useful calculator, not a sales pitch.

The first 100 users plan will be tightened after interviews. The strongest likely channel is Credex-specific: Credex can publish anonymized real savings teardown posts based on common startup AI stacks and point readers to the audit.
